Data Scale & Engineering Constraints

Note on Scale: This pipeline operates in a low-resource environment, utilizing a compact training corpus of 1,000 labeled tweets. While modest in scale, this constraint necessitates a focus on feature density and model interpretability—making linear classification an optimal choice to prevent overfitting while maintaining high predictive transparency.

This system was developed within a deliberately constrained data environment. Rather than relying on massive corpora, the model is trained on a compact, carefully labeled dataset representative of short-form social media discourse. This scale foregrounds methodological discipline: feature design, statistical transparency, and overfitting prevention become central engineering concerns.

In low-resource settings, model complexity must be balanced against interpretability. A linear classification framework was selected to preserve coefficient-level explainability while maintaining generalization performance. This design ensures that predictive behavior remains traceable to measurable input signals rather than opaque parameter interactions.

The primary technical challenge lies in fusing dual sentiment channels: linguistic structure and symbolic expression. By integrating TF-IDF textual statistics with engineered emoji polarity features, the pipeline constructs a unified representation capable of capturing emotional nuance even under data constraints.

With the polarity mechanism validated in Step 4, this stage asks how far it can be pushed. Three independent signal sources are engineered, each justified by corpus evidence, and stacked into a single production feature matrix that features.py implements exactly.

1

Expanded Emoji Polarity

Lexicon expanded from 6 to 193 emojis — closing the 15.2% corpus coverage gap identified in EDA. Produces emoji_pos_count and emoji_neg_count via character-level lookup.

0.834 F1

+0.038 vs baseline

2

Emoticon Fallback

Western-style emoticons (:), :(, :D) are invisible to character-level emoji extraction. Substring matching closes this structural gap with a separate pos/neg count pair.

0.838 F1

+0.042 vs baseline

3

Word Lexicon

On a 1,000-sample corpus, TF-IDF may underweight low-frequency but high-signal words. A deterministic lexicon (happy, love, hate, terrible…) injects stable priors as word_pos_count and word_neg_count, present in 50.1% and 29.0% of tweets respectively.

0.840 F1

+0.044 vs baseline

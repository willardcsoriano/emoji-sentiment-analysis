Under the Hood

The model is not a black box. Every prediction is the result of measurable arithmetic — token weights, polarity counts, a dot product, and a deterministic veto check. Three worked examples, from raw text to numerical output.

{# Algorithm Explainer — How the Model Works Sits between the Under the Hood header and the collapsible worked examples. #}
Inference Pipeline · Logistic Regression

Every prediction follows the same five-step sequence. No neural network — a dot product, a sigmoid, and one deterministic override rule.

01

Feature Extraction

Text is vectorized into 1,265 TF-IDF dimensions (token frequency weighted by corpus rarity), then appended with 4 hand-engineered polarity counts — emoji_pos, emoji_neg, word_pos, word_neg — each multiplied by BOOST=10 to compete against the TF-IDF dimensions. Total: 1,269 features.

02

Dot Product → Logit

Each feature is multiplied by its learned coefficient and summed into one number — the logit. Coefficients were fixed at training time. The dominant one: word_neg_count = −4.1277, the strongest predictor found during ablation.

logit = (w₁×f₁) + (w₂×f₂) + … + (w₁₂₆₉×f₁₂₆₉) + bias
03

Sigmoid → Confidence

Sigmoid squashes the logit into (0, 1). Above 0.5 → Positive. Below → Negative. Output is two probabilities that always sum to 1.0 — one per class. Confidence below 0.70 raises the High Ambiguity flag.

0.731

logit 0

0.500

04

Sarcasm Veto

Before the model runs, a deterministic rule checks for emoji-text conflict. If emoji_neg ≥ 10 and the model would predict Positive, the veto bypasses the model entirely — no dot product, no sigmoid — and returns a hardcoded Negative with confidence scaled by signal dominance (0.75–0.95). One guard suppresses it when word_pos ≥ 2 and emoji_pos = 0, deferring to the model for genuine emotional overflow.

05

Driver Extraction

Every non-zero feature is collected, its coefficient attached, and sorted by absolute value. The top 3 are the Top Drivers. Note: token coefficients reflect training co-occurrence, not dictionary meaning — "love" carries −0.5021 because it appeared frequently in sarcastic negative tweets. The lexicon features exist precisely to correct for this.
